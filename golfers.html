The captain of Team Europe. Biz remains one the Ryder Cup's most skilled golfers. He will have his hands full this tournament with matchup decisions and lineup choices, but that won't stop him from shooting low.

Barnes

HCP: / Shoots: Right

Individual RC Record: 7-8-1

Team RC Record: 3-0-1

Tyler Barnes is a golfer you hear before you see—because by the time his drive lands, it’s already 40 yards past yours. Known around the course as “Mr. 305” for his effortless power off the tee, Tyler plays the game with a mix of swagger and unpredictability that keeps every round interesting. By day, he works as a dedicated physician’s assistant, bringing care and precision to his patients—though that same precision doesn’t always translate to his scorecard… or his spelling. Scorekeeping with Tyler can be an adventure in itself, as his handwriting and word choices occasionally require a translator. What Tyler lacks in orthographic accuracy, he more than makes up for in raw distance and confidence. Whether he’s bombing drives, talking trash with a grin, or trying to spell “birdie” (and getting close enough), Tyler Barnes is a memorable presence on any course.

Busch

HCP: / Shoots: Right

Individual RC Record: 6-7-3

Team RC Record: 3-0-1

Busch is a familiar sight on the course—mostly because he’s always there. If there’s a tee time open, he’s in it. If there isn’t, he’s probably trying to create one. His dedication to the game is unmatched, and it shows in a swing that’s as consistent as his presence in the group. Simply put, he’s one of the best sticks in the lineup. Off the course, Busch has a well-known zero-tolerance policy for dandelions. A single yellow dot in the yard is enough to ruin his day, and his lawn care standards are almost as precise as his short game. On the course, though, that precision sometimes gives way to indecision—Busch can flip-flop at any moment. But when it’s time to swing, all that second-guessing fades away. He steps up, commits, and delivers. Whether he’s grinding out pars or sticking approach shots close, Busch proves time and again that he’s the one everyone’s chasing on the leaderboard.

Matt spent last year battling a hitch/pump in his golf swing that looked like it needed its own timeout between backswing and downswing. This season? Percy

HCP: / Shoots: Left

Individual RC Record: 10-3-3

Team RC Record: 1-2-1

Percy is the proud father of a daughter and the proud owner of the most boring golf game in the league. While others chase hero shots and viral highlights, Percy prefers the revolutionary strategy of hitting fairways, finding greens, and quietly taking everyone's money. Fresh off the acquisition of a new putter—his latest attempt to fix a problem that may or may not have existed—Percy enters each season with renewed confidence and a marginally better short game. Opponents rarely leave a Percy match talking about excitement, but they often leave talking about a loss. Despite generating all the drama of a tax seminar, his Ryder Cup record speaks volumes. Match after match, Percy methodically grinds opponents into submission, proving that boring golf is only boring when you're not winning. Consistent, steady, and annoyingly effective, he's become one of the toughest points on the board to take down.

Puddy

HCP: / Shoots: Right

Individual RC Record: 8-7-1

Team RC Record: 1-2-1

Few athletes have ever experienced the kind of heater Puddy is currently riding. Whether it's women, concerts, cold drinks, or somehow all three at the same time, he's in the middle of a generational run that deserves its own documentary. On the golf course, Puddy is a walking contradiction. His driver has absolutely no allegiance to the fairway and often appears to be operating under its own set of instructions. Yet somehow, once he gets within 100 yards, he transforms into a magician. His short game is elite, capable of turning double bogeys into pars and opponents' confidence into panic. Never far from a fresh lip pillow and always ready for a good time, Puddy brings an unmatched combination of vibes and volatility to every Ryder Cup. You never know where the drive is going, but you can be sure he'll find a way to make par, make a putt, and make a memory before the day is over. Simply put: life is good, the short game is better, and Puddy isn't cooling off anytime soon.

Known throughout the tournament as "The Midnight Gunner," Todd is equipped with grips so large they should require special permitting. Fortunately, his hands are large enough to handle them. Despite this, Todd has been playing some lights out golf and is quickly becoming someone who can flip the script in this tournament. When the lights are brightest, the Gunner is usually firing.

Pete

HCP: / Shoots: Right

Individual RC Record: 9-5-2

Team RC Record: 1-2-1

Pete is all about balance - father, a top performer at Modern Wealth Management, most winnigest at something and living proof that a steady hand can build both portfolios and Ryder Cup victories. Around the course, he's known for two things: an unwavering loyalty to Coors Light and a 7-wood that should probably be tested for legality. While most golfers use the club as an occasional utility option, Pete treats it like a secret weapon. Fairway, rough, trouble, pressure—it doesn't matter. If the 7-wood is in his hands, opponents know something good is about to happen. Affectionately known as "Puttin' Pete," he's developed a reputation for burying clutch putts at exactly the wrong time for whoever happens to be standing across from him. Few players are more comfortable when a match comes down to six feet and bragging rights. His Ryder Cup record reflects the same consistency he brings to his professional life: dependable, productive, and quietly dangerous. Pete may not be the loudest player in the field, but by the end of the weekend, he's usually collected points, cashed putts, and cracked open a well-earned Coors Light. When the pressure rises, Puttin' Pete tends to do what he always does—find the fairway, pull the 7-wood, and make the putt.
